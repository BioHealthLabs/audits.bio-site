Oversight · Integrity · Accuracy · Compliance

Independent audits ensuring accuracy, neutrality and compliance across all ronarn AS websites.

Audits.bio documents the review procedures that keep the network trustworthy: verifying data, evidence quality, affiliate neutrality, ranking logic, policy compliance and update schedules.

  • Accuracy checks for ingredient, health and product data.
  • Neutrality audits for affiliate-supported pages.
  • Validation of comparison tables and ranking logic.
  • Oversight of updates, evidence revisions and policy compliance.

Works in parallel with Verification.bio, Standards.bio and Transparency.bio.

1. Purpose of the Audit Framework

The ronarn AS network includes educational and comparison websites across skincare, DNA testing, nutrition and health topics. Audits.bio ensures all content meets strict requirements for:

  • accuracy
  • neutrality
  • relevance
  • policy compliance
  • evidence quality

2. Types of Audits Performed

The audit system is divided into four major categories:

  • Content accuracy audits – verifying ingredient facts, product data & science references.
  • Affiliate neutrality audits – ensuring commercial partnerships do not influence ranking order or recommendations.
  • Ranking & scoring audits – checking consistency and fairness across comparison tables.
  • Policy compliance audits – ensuring all pages follow privacy, disclosure and transparency rules.

3. Update & Review Schedules

Every domain in the network follows a rolling audit plan:

  • Monthly: affiliate status, broken links, retailer availability.
  • Quarterly: product data, price/ML calculations, photos, ingredient lists.
  • Every 6 months: ranking logic, formula changes, category definitions.
  • Every 12–18 months: scientific evidence refresh.

4. Oversight & Governance Structure

Audits.bio is part of the governance tree:

  • Transparency.bio – public clarity
  • Disclosure.bio – affiliate declarations
  • Policies.bio – unified policies
  • Verification.bio – data integrity
  • Standards.bio – editorial standards
  • Audits.bio – oversight & inspection

Together they form a complete compliance, transparency and quality framework.

5. Audit Triggers

Extra audits are automatically triggered when:

  • a major product formula changes
  • a new competitor enters the category
  • evidence changes (new studies published)
  • affiliate partnerships change
  • a product becomes unavailable or is discontinued

6. Corrective Actions

When an audit finds an issue, corrective actions may include:

  • re-scoring rankings
  • updating product tables
  • rewriting outdated claims
  • adjusting affiliate disclosures
  • removing non-compliant content
